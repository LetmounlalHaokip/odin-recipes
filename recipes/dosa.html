Dosa

Description

Dosa is a beloved South Indian dish, particularly from Tamil Nadu and Karnataka, celebrated for its crispy, thin pancake-like texture. Made from a fermented rice and lentil batter, this savory crepe is often served with coconut chutney and sambar, a lentil-based vegetable stew.

Ingredients

  • Rice: 1.5 cups, preferably parboiled or idli rice, soaked for 6 hours.
  • Urad Dal (Split Black Gram): ½ cup, soaked for 6 hours.
  • Fenugreek Seeds: 1 tsp, soaked with urad dal to enhance fermentation.
  • Salt: 1 tsp, or to taste.
  • Oil or Ghee: 2-3 tbsp for cooking dosas.Oil or Ghee: 2-3 tbsp for cooking dosas.

Steps to Cook

  1. Prepare Batter: Drain soaked rice and urad dal, then blend separately with a little water into smooth pastes; mix both with fenugreek seeds and salt in a bowl.Prepare Batter: Drain soaked rice and urad dal, then blend separately with a little water into smooth pastes; mix both with fenugreek seeds and salt in a bowl.
  2. Ferment Batter: Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it becomes frothy and slightly sour.
  3. Heat Tawa: Preheat a flat non-stick tawa or griddle on medium heat, lightly grease with oil.
  4. Spread Dosa: Pour a ladle of batter onto the tawa, spread thinly in a circular motion, and drizzle ½ tsp oil around the edges.
  5. Cook and Serve: Cook until golden and crisp (2-3 minutes), flip if desired, then fold and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
